Ramen

Description

Tonkatsu ramen is a rich and creamy Japanese noodle soup made from pork bones simmered over many hours to extract deep flavor and collagen, resulting in a hearty, milky broth. To make it, pork bones (like neck bones or trotters) are boiled briefly, cleaned, then simmered with aromatics like onion, garlic, and ginger for 6 to 12 hours. The long cooking time breaks down the bones and marrow, creating that signature thick and flavorful base. This broth is then combined with a tare—a simple seasoning sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, and sake—that gives the soup its salty umami punch.

When serving, hot broth is ladled over ramen noodles and topped with classic garnishes like slices of chashu pork, soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and sometimes corn or bamboo shoots. The result is a deeply satisfying bowl of ramen that’s warming, savory, and packed with layers of flavor and texture. Though it takes time, the process is straightforward and well worth the effort for a comforting homemade bowl of tonkatsu ramen.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork bones (neck bones or trotters work well)
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 5 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 knob (2-inch) ginger, sliced
  • 2 green onions (optional)
  • Water (enough to cover bones)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sake (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar

Steps

Prepare the broth (can be made a day ahead)

  1. Rinse pork bones under cold water
  2. Place bones in a large pot, fill with water, and bring to a boil
  3. Once boiling, drain the pot and rinse the bones to remove scum
  4. Return clean bones to the pot. Add onion, garlic, ginger, and green onions
  5. Fill with fresh water to cover the bones
  6. Simmer uncovered for 6–12 hours, adding water as needed. The longer it simmers, the creamier and richer it gets

Make the Tare

  1. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar
  2. Stir until sugar dissolves. Set aside

Prepare Ramen Noodles

  1. Boil ramen noodles according the package instructions
  2. Drain and set aside

Assemble the Ramen

  1. Add 1-2 tbsp of tare to the bottom of each bowl.
  2. Ladle in the hot broth and stir to combine.
  3. Add ramen noodles.
  4. Top with pork slices, soft-boiled egg halves, green onions, nori, and any other toppings you like.
